Development of Solid Hydrogen Dressings for Health Care Products

March 5, 2025 · Martin Chen · Research

Applying solid-state hydrogen technology to sanitary napkins, diapers, and panty liners — a new frontier in health care.

Solid hydrogen dressings apply solid-state hydrogen technology to hygiene care products — sanitary napkins, diapers, and panty liners. Hydrogen's selective antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties offer a new dimension of health protection.

With the rapid development of hydrogen medicine and solid-state hydrogen storage technology, it has become possible to apply solid-state hydrogen technology to hygiene care products. This represents a convergence of advanced materials science with everyday health care needs.

Technical Background

Hydrogen Medicine Foundation

Hydrogen gas (H₂) as a selective antioxidant, anti-inflammatory factor, and immune regulator has been proven to have potential improvement effects on various chronic diseases and inflammation. Hydrogen molecules can penetrate cell membranes, neutralize harmful reactive oxygen species (ROS), reduce oxidative stress damage, and alleviate chronic inflammation by inhibiting pathways such as NF-κB.

Solid-State Hydrogen Storage

Solid-state hydrogen technology stabilizes hydrogen gas in solid carriers through physical adsorption or chemical bonding. Metal hydrides (magnesium-based materials) and mineral ceramic carriers are the most common substrates, offering controlled release and extended hydrogen generation.
